Design and Develop a Community-Controlled Game

I would like to invite you to take part in a research study. Before you decide you need to understand why the research is being done and what it would involve for you. Please take time to read the following information carefully. Ask questions if anything you read is not clear or would like more information. Take time to decide whether or not to take part.

 

What is the purpose of the study?

To gather feedback on a community-controlled game, and to playtest it for experience and technical purposes.

 

Why have I been invited?

You have been invited to participate in this study because you play video games on occasion. You have also been invited since you fall within the age group of 16-30. This is because you may have some reasonable experience playing video games, and can provide relevant and adequate feedback using your experiences as a basis.

 

Do I have to take part?

It is up to you to decide. We will describe the study and go through the information sheet, which we will give to you. We will then ask you to sign a consent form to show you agreed to take part. You are free to withdraw at any time, without giving a reason.

 

What will happen to me if I take part?

You will be required to participate in a game session for at least 10 minutes. This gameplay session may also be recorded visually.

 

You are allowed to withdraw from any session without giving any reason.

 

You will also be expected to provide feedback in the form of a questionnaire, giving as much feedback as possible.

 

Debug information (information that is provided by the application for testing purposes) may be logged, but you will only be identified using a randomly generated ID unique to your session, which cannot be linked to you in any way.

 

What will I have to do?

You will be expected to briefly play a game and give feedback in the form of questionnaires. Alternatively, feedback can also be given in the form of a couple of sentences should you have any minor issues during the session itself.

 

The game is a browser-based game, meaning that you are expected to play the game in Google Chrome or Firefox. Microsoft Edge and Internet Explorer are browsers that will not be used in this study.

 

What are the possible disadvantages and risks of taking part?

General risks of prolonged computer use apply, such as RSI, other minor muscle issues, and eye strain.

 

What are the possible benefits of taking part?

We cannot promise the study will help you but the information we get from the study will help to increase the understanding of community-controlled gameplay.

 

What if there is a problem?

If you have a concern about any aspect of this study, you should ask to speak to the researchers who will do their best to answer your questions. Please contact 17098897@stu.mmu.ac.uk for this.

 

Will my taking part in the study be kept confidential?

All information which is collected about you during the course of the research will be kept strictly confidential, and any information about you which leaves the university will have your name and address removed so that you cannot be recognised.

 

What will happen if I don't carry on with the study?

If you withdraw from the study all the information and data collected from you, to date, will be destroyed and your name removed from all the study files.

 

What will happen to the results of the research study?

The feedback provided will be used to change the experience of the game. Your feedback will remain anonymous and will not be published.

 

 

Further information and contact details:

It is advised that you avoid using mobile devices or computers for at least 10 minutes before the study to avoid any physical health issues associated with overuse of electronic devices.

 

Should you have any complaints or questions, please contact 17098897@stu.mmu.ac.uk by email.
